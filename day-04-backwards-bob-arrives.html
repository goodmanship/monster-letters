Backwards Bob Arrives

Day 4 • Week 1
📖 Read aloud

A new monster came to Scribble's house — Backwards Bob! Bob is really nice, but he does everything backwards. He walked in through the back door, said "BYE" instead of "HI," and then wrote Scribble a note — but it was all backwards!

Can you help decode Bob's note?

Bob's note to Scribble
IH ELBBRICS! I MA BOB.
🔍 Decode it! What does Bob's note really say?
🧩 For parents

Read the story aloud with energy! When you get to the mirror note, let him try to figure it out. Hints: "What if you read each word backwards?" The answer is: "HI SCRIBBLE! I AM BOB." If he gets stuck, work through the first word together, then let him try the rest. This is playful decoding, not a test.

What's new today

New character — Backwards Bob! Bob is tall, blue, friendly, and confused. He does everything in reverse. Today introduces the concept of letter reversals through a character who mixes everything up — making it funny instead of frustrating. The mirror-writing note is a playful decoding activity, not a handwriting test.

Phase 1 — The mirror note

Bob's note reads "IH ELBBRICS! I MA BOB." which decodes to "HI SCRIBBLE! I AM BOB." Work through it together. You can hold the page up to a mirror for a fun "aha!" moment, or just reverse each word letter by letter. Let him write the decoded version in the lines below the note. This is warm-up writing disguised as puzzle-solving.

Phase 2 — Letter Warm-Up (b/d/p/q)

Today's letter drill focuses on commonly reversed letters: b, d, p, q. The framing is that Bob mixes these up because he does everything backwards. Your child writes each letter 5 times fast. Do not correct reversals during the drill. Speed and repetition build the motor memory that prevents reversals naturally. If he reverses a letter, the reps themselves are the fix.

Phase 2 — Bob's Backwards Words

Dictation format — you say the word forward, he writes it forward. Words: SIT, LIME, HEN, JAM, WEB, ZIP, FUN, VAN. The joke is that Bob would write them all backwards, but your child is "fixing" them. Same scoring as previous days: 1 point for attempting, bonus for readable. Track the week total — tomorrow is the last day to set records!

Phase 3 — The challenge

He writes Bob a note explaining how to walk into a house correctly. The prompt template ("Dear Bob, first you go to the _____ door. Then you say _____.") gives structure, but he can write his own version. 2–3 sentences is the target. The drawing prompt asks him to draw Bob walking in backwards — this is a reward for finishing the writing, and kids love drawing monsters doing silly things.

Day 4 Wins

  • He decoded Bob's mirror note (with help is fine!)
  • He attempted all 4 reversal letters (b, d, p, q)
  • He attempted all 8 dictation words
  • He wrote at least 2 sentences to Bob
  • Session stayed under 15 minutes

Watch for

  • Anxiety about b/d reversals — "Bob gets them wrong too, and he's fine!"
  • Trying to write the mirror note backwards himself — fun but save it for later
  • Getting stuck on the challenge — the template is there to help, use it!
  • Spending too long on the drawing — drawing is the reward, but keep the timer going
💬 Looking ahead

Tomorrow (Day 5) is the Monster Party — the Week 1 finale! All three monsters throw a party, and your child does all the writing. He'll revisit his letter-sprint records from Day 2 and try to beat them. It's a celebration day. If he's been tracking scores, tomorrow is when they all pay off.
