# Monster Letters: Month 1 Curriculum

## The Cast

**Scribble** — A fuzzy purple monster. Enthusiastic but messy. Writes letters to your son constantly, but they're full of mistakes and hard to read. Needs help writing things clearly. Scribble's motto: "Close enough!"

**Speedster** — A small green monster with lightning bolts on his ears. Does everything too fast. Sends messages that need to be rewritten because he rushed. Always in a hurry. Catchphrase: "GOGOGOGO!"

**Backwards Bob** — A tall blue monster who does everything in reverse. Writes words backwards, reads books from the end, puts his shoes on the wrong feet. Sweet and confused.

**Captain Careful** — A tiny orange monster who is SO careful that he never finishes anything. Introduced in Week 3. He spends so long on his first letter that he never gets to the second one. The other monsters love him but try to help him speed up. (This is the gentle mirror character — not mocking, but showing the humor in over-checking, and eventually showing Captain Careful learning to let go.)

---

## How to Run a Session

Each session is **15 minutes, 3 phases.** Do 4-5 sessions per week (weekdays, skip when life happens). You need:

- A timer (phone is fine)
- A pencil and lined paper (or a dedicated notebook — "The Monster Letters Notebook")
- A die or a deck of letter cards (index cards with one letter each)
- These prompts

**Your role:** You read the story setup aloud (or he reads it if he wants). You run the timer. You do NOT comment on handwriting quality during speed drills. After the session, you can pick ONE letter to practice formation on separately if needed — but keep that completely separate from this.

## WEEK 1: "Scribble Needs Help"

**Story Arc:** Scribble just moved to Monster Town and needs to label everything in his new house, but his writing is so messy that nobody can read his labels. He keeps putting "BED" on the stove and "LAMP" on the toilet. Your son is the only human who can help.

---

### Day 1 — Scribble's First Letter

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
Read aloud or tell in your own words:
> "A purple monster named Scribble just moved into a new house in Monster Town. He tried to write labels for everything, but his writing is SO messy that he put the wrong label on everything! He put 'BED' on the refrigerator and 'SINK' on the couch. He needs your help to write clear labels. He sent you this letter..."

Write a short messy note in your own handwriting (make it intentionally sloppy/silly):
> "DEER FREND, I NEED HELP. MY HOUS IS A MESS. KAN YOU HELP ME LABL THINGS? — SKRIBL"

Have your son read it together with you (decoding the bad spelling is part of the fun).

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Letter Warm-Up (2 min)*
Flip through letter cards (or roll a die and count through the alphabet). Whatever letter comes up, he writes it 5 times fast. Do 6-8 letters. Timer running. No erasing allowed.

*Drill 2: Label Blitz (5 min)*
Name items in a house. He writes the word as fast as he can. One word at a time, you say the next one when he's done. Don't wait for perfection.

Words: BED, CUP, RUG, PAN, TUB, MOP, HAT, BOX

Score: 1 point per word attempted, bonus point if you can read it. Tell him his score enthusiastically.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Scribble needs a letter back! Your son writes:
> "HI SCRIBBLE. I WILL HELP."

That's it. Two sentences. If he finishes fast, he can draw a picture of what he thinks Scribble looks like.

---

### Day 2 — The Kitchen Labels

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Scribble says thanks for writing back! Today he needs help with the kitchen. He tried to label things but look what happened..."

Show him a piece of paper where you've written (messily):
> WINDOW → (on a drawing of a stove)
> CHAIR → (on a drawing of a sink)
> DOG → (on a drawing of a cup)

Let him laugh at how wrong it all is.

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Letter Sprint (2 min)*
Pick 3 letters he finds tricky (b, d, g, q, 5, etc.). One line of each, as fast as he can. Time each line. Write down his times — these are his "records" to beat later.

*Drill 2: Kitchen Words (5 min)*
Dictation — you say the word, he writes it. Go at his pace, but encourage speed.

Words: CUP, POT, PAN, LID, JAR, MUG, DISH, FORK

Score the same way: 1 for attempt, bonus for readable.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write the correct labels for Scribble. Draw three boxes (or he draws them). He writes the right word in each box:
- STOVE
- SINK
- CUP

If he finishes early: "Write Scribble one sentence of advice about his kitchen."

---

### Day 3 — Speedster Shows Up

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Scribble's friend Speedster came to visit! Speedster is a little green monster who does everything SO FAST. He ran into the kitchen, knocked over all the labels, and wrote new ones — but he went so fast that the words are all jumbled!"

Write these on a card (messy, fast-looking handwriting):
> UPC (CUP), OTP (POT), ROFK (FORK)

Have your son decode them.

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Speedster Challenge (3 min)*
"Speedster says he can write any letter 10 times in 15 seconds. Can you beat him?"

Pick 5 letters. For each: set timer for 15 seconds, he writes as many of that letter as he can. Count them up. Tell him Speedster got 9 — did he beat Speedster?

*Drill 2: Word Fix (4 min)*
You write scrambled words (like Speedster did). He figures out the real word and writes it correctly.

Scrambles: OGD (DOG), TAC (CAT), NUS (SUN), THA (HAT), GUB (BUG), DAM (MAD)

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
"Speedster wants to race you! He says he can write this sentence faster than you can."

The sentence: THE CUP IS ON THE RUG.

Your son writes it. Time him. Tell him Speedster's time was 1 second slower than whatever he gets. He wins!

Then: "Write one sentence telling Speedster to slow down."

---

### Day 4 — Backwards Bob Arrives

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "A new monster came to Scribble's house — Backwards Bob! Bob is really nice, but he does everything backwards. He walked in through the back door, said 'BYE' instead of 'HI,' and then wrote Scribble a note — but it was all backwards!"

Write a note in mirror writing (just reverse each word):
> IH ELBBRICS! I MA BOB.

Help your son decode it in the mirror (or just by figuring it out).

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Letter Warm-Up (2 min)*
Letter cards, 5 each, fast. Focus: any letters he tends to reverse (b/d, p/q). No correction during drill — just reps. Speed builds the motor memory that kills the reversals over time.

*Drill 2: Bob's Backwards Words (5 min)*
You say a word forward. He writes it forward (correctly). The joke is that Bob would write it backwards, but your son is helping fix it.

Words: SIT, MOP, HEN, JAM, WEB, ZIP, FUN, VAN

Same scoring. Track his total for the week.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write Bob a note explaining how to walk into a house the RIGHT way. Two to three sentences. Example prompt:
> "Dear Bob, first you go to the _____ door. Then you say _____."

He fills in the blanks or writes his own version.

---

### Day 5 — The Monster Party

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "It's Friday, so the monsters are having a party! Scribble, Speedster, and Backwards Bob are all at Scribble's house. They need to make a sign for the party and write invitations — but every time one of them tries to write, it goes wrong! They need YOU to do all the writing."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Personal Record Attempt (3 min)*
Go back to the 3 letters from Day 2 that he timed. Do them again. Can he beat his records? Celebrate any improvement.

*Drill 2: Party Words (4 min)*
Words: FUN, CAKE, GAME, SING, JUMP, GIFT, PLAY, GRIN

Dictation, fast pace. Score it up. Compare to Day 1 total — he should see improvement, which feels great.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Two tasks, his choice which to do (or both):

1. **Party Sign:** Write "MONSTER PARTY" in big letters and decorate it.
2. **Invitation:** Write a 2-sentence invitation. Example: "Come to the party! It will be fun."

**End of Week 1 — Weekly Record:**
Add up all his drill scores for the week. Write the total on a "Week 1" card. This is the number to beat next week.

---

## WEEK 2: "Monster Town Problems"

**Story Arc:** The monsters start exploring Monster Town, but there are problems everywhere that require writing to solve — signs are missing, the mail got mixed up, the grocery list is wrong. Each day is a new mini-adventure.

---

### Day 6 — Missing Street Signs

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "The monsters went for a walk in Monster Town, but somebody stole all the street signs! The monsters keep getting lost. Scribble ended up at the dump. Speedster ran into a lake. Bob walked backwards into a tree. They need you to make new street signs!"

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Capital Letter Sprint (3 min)*
Street signs need capitals! Flash a letter card — he writes the CAPITAL version 3 times fast. Do 8-10 letters.

*Drill 2: Street Name Dictation (4 min)*
He writes these street names as you say them. Two-word combos push him a little.

Names: ELM ST, OAK AVE, BIG HILL RD, MUD LANE, SUN WAY, FOG DR

Score: 1 point per word.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Make a street sign! He picks his favorite street name (or invents one) and writes it in big, clear letters. Then write one sentence of directions for the monsters:
> "Go down _____ to get to the _____."

---

### Day 7 — Mixed-Up Mail

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Speedster got a job at the Monster Town Post Office, but he's SO fast that he mixed up all the mail! Scribble got Bob's letter. Bob got a letter for someone named Greta. And a monster named Fuzz got Scribble's electric bill. They need you to rewrite the addresses so the mail goes to the right monsters."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Name Writing Speed (3 min)*
Write each monster's name 3 times fast: SCRIBBLE, BOB, SPEEDSTER, FUZZ, GRETA
(These are longer words — good progression from Week 1's short words.)

*Drill 2: Address Dictation (4 min)*
You dictate, he writes. Pushing into multi-word phrases now.

Addresses:
- BOB, 5 OAK ST
- SCRIBBLE, 12 MUD LANE
- GRETA, 8 SUN WAY
- FUZZ, 3 ELM ST

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a short letter FROM one monster TO another. 2-3 sentences. Prompt:
> "Scribble wants to write to Greta and say sorry for opening her mail. Help him write it."

Example: "Dear Greta, I am sorry I got your mail. Speedster mixed it up. From, Scribble"

---

### Day 8 — The Grocery Disaster

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Scribble went to the monster grocery store, but he lost his list! He's trying to remember what he needs. He keeps calling you to ask how to spell things. Also, Backwards Bob came along and keeps putting things in the cart backwards — like putting bananas in a bag upside down."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Speed Review (2 min)*
Pick his 3 weakest letters from the past week. One line of each, timed. Beat the record?

*Drill 2: Grocery List Blitz (5 min)*
You say items, he writes them. Moving into slightly harder words.

Words: MILK, EGGS, RICE, FISH, CORN, PLUM, NUTS, LIME, BREAD, JAM

Challenge scoring: Bonus 2 points for any word with 4+ letters that's readable.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write your OWN grocery list for a monster. What would a monster eat? He writes a list of 5+ items. Encourage silly/creative items:
- SLIME SOUP
- BAT WINGS
- MUD PIE
- BUG JUICE

The sillier the better — silly gets him writing without thinking.

---

### Day 9 — The Lost Pet

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Oh no! Scribble's pet snail, Gary, is missing! The monsters need to make 'LOST PET' signs to put up around Monster Town. Speedster tried to make one but it just says 'LST PT' because he went too fast. Bob tried but his sign says 'DNUOF — LIANS TSOL.' They need you!"

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Sentence Sprint (3 min)*
New drill type! You say a short sentence, he writes the whole thing. Time it.

Sentences:
1. THE SNAIL IS LOST.
2. HIS NAME IS GARY.
3. HE IS GREEN.

Time each one. These are his sentence speed records.

*Drill 2: Description Words (4 min)*
Dictation — words you'd use to describe a pet:
SMALL, SLOW, GREEN, SOFT, CUTE, ROUND, TINY, SLIMY

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Make a LOST PET sign! Must include:
- LOST PET at the top
- The pet's name
- What it looks like (1-2 sentences)
- Where to bring it if found (1 sentence)

He can draw Gary the snail too if time permits.

---

### Day 10 — Gary Is Found!

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Great news! A monster named Dot found Gary the snail under a mushroom in Monster Town Park! She saw your signs and knew just who to call. Scribble is SO happy. He wants to write thank-you notes to everyone who helped look — but of course, he needs YOUR help writing them."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Week 2 Record Attempt (3 min)*
Pick any 3 drills from this week — do a final timed attempt. Can he set a new personal best on any of them?

*Drill 2: Feeling Words (4 min)*
Dictation of words that describe emotions (new category — he'll need these for writing):
HAPPY, SAD, MAD, GLAD, BRAVE, SCARED, KIND, PROUD

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a thank-you note from Scribble to Dot. 3+ sentences. Prompt:
> "Help Scribble say thank you. Tell Dot where Gary was found and how happy Scribble is."

Example: "Dear Dot, Thank you for finding Gary. He was under a mushroom. I am so happy! From, Scribble"

**End of Week 2 — Weekly Record:**
Total up scores. Compare to Week 1. Celebrate the improvement.

---

## WEEK 3: "Captain Careful"

**Story Arc:** A new monster arrives — Captain Careful. He's tiny, orange, and he takes SO LONG to write every letter that he never finishes anything. The other monsters try to help him learn to go faster. (This is the therapeutic week — your son will recognize himself in Captain Careful, and by rooting for Captain Careful to speed up, he gives himself permission to do the same.)

**Important parent note:** Don't draw the comparison out loud. Don't say "he's like you!" Kids figure it out on their own, and it's more powerful when they do. Just tell the story straight.

---

### Day 11 — Captain Careful Arrives

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "A new monster moved to Monster Town! His name is Captain Careful. He's really small and orange, and he wears a tiny cape. He wants to write a letter to introduce himself to everyone, but he's been working on it for THREE DAYS and he's only written 'Dear.' He keeps erasing and starting over because the 'D' doesn't look right. Scribble went to check on him and Captain Careful said, 'I'll be done soon, I just need to fix this one letter.' That was yesterday."

(Tell it warmly and with humor — Captain Careful is lovable, not pathetic.)

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: "Help Captain Careful" Speed Round (3 min)*
"Captain Careful has been trying to write these letters for an hour. Let's show him how fast you can do it!"

Pick 6 letters. He writes each one 5 times, fast. The framing: he's demonstrating for Captain Careful that fast is okay.

*Drill 2: Intro Words (4 min)*
Words Captain Careful is trying to use in his letter:
HELLO, MY, NAME, IS, I, LIKE, LIVE, PLAY, NICE, MEET

Dictation, score as usual.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write the introduction letter that Captain Careful can't finish. 3+ sentences.
> "Hi, my name is Captain Careful. I live in Monster Town. I like _____."

He's literally finishing what Captain Careful couldn't — because he wrote FAST enough.

---

### Day 12 — Captain Careful's Bad Day

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Captain Careful tried to write a grocery list today. He spent 20 minutes writing 'MILK' because he kept erasing the 'M.' Then he spent 10 minutes on the 'I' in 'EGGS' because — wait, there's no 'I' in 'EGGS.' He checked so many times that he got confused and thought there was! Speedster tried to help by grabbing the pencil and writing the whole list in 2 seconds, but Captain Careful couldn't read it. They need someone who can write FAST but also CLEAR."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Fast AND Clear Challenge (3 min)*
New scoring: Speed score (how many in 30 seconds) PLUS clarity score (how many are readable). This bridges the gap — it's not just about speed anymore, but it's never about perfection.

Do 4 letter rounds with this new scoring.

*Drill 2: Grocery List Race (4 min)*
You and your son BOTH write the same grocery list at the same time. You write with your non-dominant hand (to make it fair and funny). Who finishes first?

Words: MILK, EGGS, RICE, HAM, CAKE, CORN, FISH, PLUM

(This is silly and competitive. Your bad handwriting gives him permission to be imperfect.)

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
"Captain Careful wants to practice going faster. Write him a pep talk! Tell him it's okay if it's not perfect."

3+ sentences. This is where the magic happens — he's articulating the lesson himself.

---

### Day 13 — The Worst Handwriting Contest

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Speedster had an idea to help Captain Careful: a WORST HANDWRITING CONTEST! Whoever writes the UGLIEST letters wins! Scribble is excited because he thinks he'll finally win something. Bob said he'd write his entry backwards AND upside down. Captain Careful is nervous — he's never written something ugly on PURPOSE before."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: The Worst Handwriting Contest (4 min)*
Actually do this. Write the sentence "THE MONSTER PARTY WAS FUN" as BADLY as possible. You do it too. Compare. Who wrote it worse? Give a trophy (draw a silly trophy on the page) to the winner.

Then write it again normally. It'll feel easy and fun by comparison.

*Drill 2: Normal Speed Dictation (3 min)*
Back to normal. Words: FRIEND, PARTY, WRITE, LETTER, MONSTER, TOWN
(Longer words this week — he's ready.)

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a news report about the Worst Handwriting Contest! 3-4 sentences.
> "Today in Monster Town, the monsters had a worst handwriting contest. _____ won because _____."

---

### Day 14 — Captain Careful Gets Faster

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Guess what? Captain Careful wrote a WHOLE SENTENCE today! It only took him 5 minutes instead of 5 hours! It's not perfect, but he did it! He wrote: 'I like my friends.' Scribble said it was the best sentence he ever read. Speedster said 'FINALLY!' Backwards Bob said '!YLLANIF!'"

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Sentence Speed Records (4 min)*
Beat the sentence records from Day 9:
1. THE SNAIL IS LOST. (old record: ____)
2. HIS NAME IS GARY. (old record: ____)
3. HE IS GREEN. (old record: ____)

New sentences to set records on:
4. I LIKE MY FRIENDS.
5. THE PARTY WAS FUN.

*Drill 2: Description Challenge (3 min)*
Dictation of 2-word phrases (adjective + noun): BIG CAKE, FAST SNAIL, BLUE HAT, OLD TREE, SAD FROG, RED STAR

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a sentence about each monster (4 sentences). One sentence each for Scribble, Speedster, Backwards Bob, and Captain Careful.

---

### Day 15 — Captain Careful's Letter (Finally!)

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Captain Careful has big news. He's going to write a letter to YOUR SON BY HIMSELF. No erasing. No starting over. He's going to do what you taught him — just GO."

Write a short note in careful-but-not-perfect handwriting (a little wobbly, but complete):
> "Dear [son's name], thank you for helping me be brave. I used to be so scared of making mistakes that I could not write anything. Now I know it does not have to be perfect. You are my friend. — Captain Careful"

Read it together.

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Week 3 Record Attempts (4 min)*
All-time best attempts on:
- Favorite letter (how many in 15 seconds)
- Favorite word drill (score)
- Fastest sentence

*Drill 2: New Word Category — Action Words (3 min)*
RUN, JUMP, SWIM, KICK, SING, DRAW, READ, HELP, PLAY, MAKE

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a letter back to Captain Careful. 3-4 sentences. No prompt — he decides what to say.

**End of Week 3 — Weekly Record + Reflection:**
Compare all 3 weeks of scores. If you want, make a simple bar chart together.

---

## WEEK 4: "The Monster Newspaper"

**Story Arc:** The monsters decide to start a newspaper for Monster Town — "The Monster Times." They need reporters, and your son is the head writer. Each day is a different section of the newspaper. This week is about creative, purposeful writing — he's writing because the newspaper NEEDS content, not because it's practice.

---

### Day 16 — Front Page News

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "The monsters are starting a newspaper! It's called 'The Monster Times.' Scribble is the artist (he draws the pictures). Speedster delivers the papers (obviously). Backwards Bob is... trying to help. And YOU are the head writer! Today you need to write the front page headline and the top story."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Headline Writing (3 min)*
You give him a headline to write in BIG letters. Speed matters — the paper goes to print soon!

Headlines to practice:
- GARY THE SNAIL FOUND
- MONSTER PARTY THIS FRIDAY
- NEW STORE IN TOWN

*Drill 2: Sentence Dictation (4 min)*
Full sentences now — he should be ready:
1. THE MONSTERS HAD A PARTY.
2. SCRIBBLE MADE A BIG CAKE.
3. BOB WALKED IN BACKWARDS.

Score: 2 points per sentence attempted, bonus for readable.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write a news story! 3-5 sentences about something that happened in Monster Town. He picks the topic or use a prompt:
> "What happened at the monster party? Write a report for the newspaper."

---

### Day 17 — The Comics Section

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Every newspaper needs a comics section! Scribble can draw the pictures, but he needs someone to write the words — the speech bubbles and captions. Today you're making a comic strip!"

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (5 min):**
(Shorter drills today — more time for the comic.)

*Drill 1: Short Phrase Sprint (2 min)*
Write short phrases that monsters might say:
OH NO!, HELP ME!, HA HA HA!, LET'S GO!, LOOK OUT!, WOW!

*Drill 2: Dialogue Practice (3 min)*
You say a line, he writes it:
1. "Hi, I am Scribble."
2. "Run, run, run!"
3. "Where is my snail?"

**Phase 3 — Challenge (8 min):**
Make a 3-panel comic strip! Draw 3 boxes. In each box, draw a simple scene (stick figures are fine!) and write what the characters say in speech bubbles. The drawing makes the writing feel less like the point — it's sneaky practice.

Prompt if needed: "Scribble and Bob go to the park. Something funny happens."

---

### Day 18 — The Advice Column

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Monsters have been writing in to the newspaper asking for advice! The advice column is called 'Ask the Human' because you're the only human in Monster Town and monsters think humans are very wise. Here are today's letters..."

Read him these "letters":
> "Dear Human, I keep walking into things because I walk backwards. What should I do? — Bob"
> "Dear Human, nobody can read my writing. How do I get better? — Scribble"

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (5 min):**

*Drill 1: Question Words (2 min)*
WHO, WHAT, WHERE, WHEN, WHY, HOW — write each 3 times fast.

*Drill 2: Advice Words (3 min)*
TRY, STOP, TURN, LOOK, SLOW, FAST, THINK, MAYBE, SHOULD, COULD

**Phase 3 — Challenge (8 min):**
Write advice back to Bob AND Scribble. 2-3 sentences each. This requires thinking AND writing — the thinking makes him forget to be careful about the writing.

---

### Day 19 — The Weather & Sports Report

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "Two more sections of the newspaper need to be filled! Speedster was supposed to write the weather report, but he just wrote 'HOT. BYE.' That's not a very good weather report. And Bob was supposed to cover the Monster Town Soccer Game but his report is backwards."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Weather Words (3 min)*
SUN, RAIN, SNOW, WIND, COLD, WARM, CLOUD, STORM

*Drill 2: Sports Sentence Sprint (4 min)*
1. THE GAME WAS CLOSE.
2. SCRIBBLE SCORED A GOAL.
3. BOB KICKED IT THE WRONG WAY.
4. THE TEAM WON!

Time each one. New records?

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write either a weather report OR a sports report (his choice). 3-4 sentences. Prompts:

Weather: "Today in Monster Town, the weather is _____. Tomorrow it will _____."
Sports: "The Monster Town _____ played today. _____ scored _____ goals."

---

### Day 20 — The Monster Times: Complete Edition!

**Phase 1 — Story (2 min):**
> "It's the last day before the newspaper goes to print! You've written the news, the comics, the advice column, and the weather. Today we're putting it all together and writing ONE more thing — the editor's note. That's where the head writer (YOU) tells the readers what to expect."

**Phase 2 — Speed Drills (7 min):**

*Drill 1: Final Record Attempts (4 min)*
All-time personal bests. Pick his 3 favorite drills from the entire month. Go for the record.

*Drill 2: Month 1 Victory Lap (3 min)*
Dictate a mix of words from all 4 weeks:
BED, FRIEND, MONSTER, LETTER, NEWSPAPER, CAPTAIN, CAREFUL, SCRIBBLE

These are WAY harder words than Week 1's CUP and BED. He can handle them now.

**Phase 3 — Challenge (5 min):**
Write the Editor's Note. 3-5 sentences. This is the most open-ended prompt yet.
> "Welcome to The Monster Times! My name is _____. In this newspaper, you will find _____. I hope you like it. — The Editor"

**End of Week 4:**

**Celebration!** Total up all the scores from the month. Compare Week 1 to Week 4. If you've been tracking records, show him how much faster he's gotten.

Optional: Compile the best pieces from the month into an actual folded "newspaper" he can show to people. This gives him a product he's PROUD of — and it's made of writing he did without agonizing over it.

## Parent Cheat Sheet

### Do:
- Run the timer on every speed drill
- Score attempts, not accuracy
- Track and celebrate personal records
- Tell the story with energy — voices help
- Write alongside him sometimes (with your non-dominant hand)
- Keep it to 15 minutes. Stop even if he's mid-sentence. Leave him wanting more.

### Don't:
- Correct handwriting during speed drills
- Say "slow down and be more careful" (ever, during this curriculum)
- Compare his writing to his sister's
- Point out reversed letters during timed work
- Push past 15 minutes even if it's going well

### If he resists:
- Day 1-3 resistance is normal. The monsters haven't hooked him yet.
- If he hates the timer, start with untimed and add the timer in Week 2.
- If he refuses to write "messy," do the Worst Handwriting Contest early.
- If he's loving it and wants more, add a bonus "free write" period where he writes whatever he wants to the monsters. But keep the core session to 15 min.

### Handwriting instruction (separate from this):
If you want to work on specific letter formation, do it as a TOTALLY separate activity — calm, untimed, maybe before bed. "Let's just practice how to make a really nice 'b'. No rush." Keep it to 5 minutes, 1-2 letters max. This stays separated from Monster Letters so the speed/flow work doesn't get contaminated with perfection pressure.

## What's Next: Month 2 Preview

Month 2 introduces:
- **Longer writing** (5-8 sentences)
- **Story writing** (beginning, middle, end)
