What's new today

Letter Sprint with records: Today introduces timed records he'll try to beat on Day 5. Pick 3 letters he finds tricky (or use b, d, g as defaults). Write the chosen letter in the left box for him. Time each line with a phone stopwatch and write the time in the circle. These numbers matter — they're his "before" snapshot.

Phase 1 — Story tips

The wrong-labels page (page 1) IS the story. Let him look at the pictures, read the wrong labels, and react. Ham it up: "He put DOG on the cup? Does Scribble even know what a dog is?!" Writing the correct labels on page 1 is a gentle warm-up before the timed drills.

Phase 2 — Kitchen Words dictation

Same format as Day 1's Label Blitz. Say the word, he writes it. Today's words are all kitchen items: CUP, POT, PAN, LID, JAR, MUG, DISH, FORK. "DISH" and "FORK" are 4 letters — a small step up. If he nails those, call it out: "Four-letter words! Nice!"

Phase 3 — The challenge

Page 4 has the three big label boxes where he writes STOVE, SINK, and CUP in clear, label-sized writing. If he finishes early, the bonus section asks for one sentence of kitchen advice to Scribble. If he doesn't get to the bonus, no problem — it's a bonus.

Day 2 Wins

  • He laughed at the wrong labels
  • He attempted all 8 kitchen words
  • You have 3 letter-sprint times recorded
  • He wrote at least one label on page 4
  • Session stayed under 15 minutes

Watch for

  • Erasing during the sprint — remind: "no erasing in speed rounds!"
  • Getting stuck on a word — say "skip it, next one!" after 20 sec
  • Comparing to Day 1 negatively — only compare scores UP
  • Wanting to "redo" a label to make it neater — it's done, move on!
💬 Looking ahead

Tomorrow (Day 3) introduces Speedster! Everything gets faster and sillier — scrambled words, speed races, and a monster who writes SO fast nobody can read it. If your son enjoyed today's timer, he'll love tomorrow.
