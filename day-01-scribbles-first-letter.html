🧩 For parents

Read the letter together. Let him decode Scribble's funny misspellings — "DEER" instead of "DEAR," "HOUS" instead of "HOUSE." The point: Scribble tried and it's charming. Mistakes are funny, not scary.

Phase 1 — Story Script

"A purple monster named Scribble just moved into a new house in Monster Town. He tried to write labels for everything, but his writing is SO messy that he put the wrong label on everything! He put BED on the refrigerator and SINK on the couch. He needs your help! He sent you this letter..."

Then read Scribble's letter together. Let him spot the misspellings. Laugh at them.

Phase 2 — How to run the drills

Drill 1 (Letter Warm-Up): Flip a card or pick a letter. He writes it 5 times in the row. Time each row if he's into it, or just keep the pace brisk. Swap the pre-printed letters for whatever he draws. "Ready? GO!" energy.

Drill 2 (Label Blitz): Say each word clearly. He writes it. Don't spell it out — let him sound it out and try. If he freezes, say "just put down what you hear." Move to the next word when he's done (or after ~20 seconds). Score generously.

Phase 3 — Challenge Tips

The example says "HI SCRIBBLE. I WILL HELP." but let him write ANYTHING to Scribble. If he writes "HI. OK." — that's two sentences, that's a win. If he finishes fast, the drawing area gives him something fun to do without more writing pressure.

Do

  • Run the timer — keep energy up
  • Score ATTEMPTS, not accuracy
  • Use an excited voice: "Ready? Go!"
  • Celebrate the score at the end
  • Stop at 15 minutes, even mid-task
  • Let him keep the sheet — it's his

Don't

  • Correct letter formation during drills
  • Say "slow down" or "be careful"
  • Erase anything for him
  • Compare to sister's writing
  • Push past 15 minutes
  • Spell words out — let him try
💡 If he resists

Day 1 resistance is normal. He hasn't met Scribble yet and doesn't know why he should care. Read the story with maximum drama and humor. If the drills feel too pressured, drop the timer today and add it on Day 2 or 3 once the characters have hooked him. The monsters are the entry point, not the worksheets.
