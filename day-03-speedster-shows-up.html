🧩 For parents

Introduce Speedster with energy: he's fast, he's loud, and he's GOGOGOGO! The jumbled labels are a fun puzzle before the timed drills. Let your child sound them out and decode them. If he needs a hint, say: "What word does this look like if you mix the letters around?"

What's new today

Speedster! This is the first new character. Play him up with energy — talk fast, be loud, say "GOGOGOGO!" Speedster normalizes speed by being comically TOO fast. The implicit lesson: going fast is fun, and even if you mess up (like Speedster), you just fix it and keep going.

Phase 1 — Jumbled labels

Page 1 has the three jumbled labels (UPC, OTP, ROFK). Let your child puzzle them out — this is a decoding game, not a test. If he's stuck, say the letters in different orders until the word clicks. Writing the correct word below is a quick warm-up. The kitchen item pictures from Day 2 make a nice callback.

Phase 2 — Speedster Challenge (page 2)

Write a letter in each "?" box (or let him pick). Set the timer for 15 seconds per round. He writes that letter as many times as he can. Count the results. Tell him Speedster's score was 9 — he'll almost certainly beat it, which feels great. If he only gets 7-8, say "Speedster barely won that one — rematch next time!" Do NOT comment on letter formation during this drill.

Phase 2 — Word Fix (page 3)

The scrambled words: OXF=FOX, TAC=CAT, NUS=SUN, THA=HAT, GUB=BUG, PUJM=JUMP. Let him work through them. If he's stuck, say the letters out loud in different orders. He writes the correct word on the line. Score: 1 point per attempt, 1 bonus for correct unscramble.

Phase 3 — The race (page 4)

The sentence is: THE CUP IS ON THE RUG. Start the timer when you say "GO!" Write his time in the left box. Then — this is the key — tell him Speedster's time was 1 second slower than whatever he got. He always wins. Then he writes one sentence telling Speedster to slow down (any sentence is fine).

Day 3 Wins

  • He decoded at least 2 jumbled labels
  • He tried all 5 Speedster Challenge rounds
  • He attempted the unscramble words
  • He wrote the race sentence
  • He "beat" Speedster (he always does)

Watch for

  • Erasing during the Speedster Challenge — "no erasing, just keep going!"
  • Frustration with scrambled words — give hints freely, it's a game
  • Wanting to rewrite the race sentence neater — the messy version counts!
  • Going over 15 minutes — stop on time even mid-task
💬 Looking ahead

Tomorrow (Day 4) introduces Backwards Bob, who does everything in reverse! The writing drills shift to correcting backwards words and writing things "the right way." It's another silly character that makes messy writing feel normal and fixable.
